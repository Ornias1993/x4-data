 Konzure  •  2 years ago  •  X4 (Home)
I can heartily agree on the UI and the fact, that the best experience is in a small ship.

I liked frigates the most in X2, and in X4 it's the M-Class Nemmesis. It's really fast even unmodded, can mount 5 Weapons (read: 4 weapons and a launcher is most versatile, or go all torpedo boat), two turrents and two drones. At least for me it feels that the next class is only medicore. Two weapons and 4 turrents don't seem to deliver the same punch. They only got more shields and hull. Still saving for the big ships, and I am really tempted to use the first mods to get real battleships into the galaxy.

The UI demishes the fun more often then not. For example dealing with your wing:
You will have a very difficult time to deploy and arrange for example your drones. Repair drones seem only to work if you got a damaged sub system - you can't tell them to slowly reapair your ship while not in combat. Neither your ship, nor others even though you can repair all ships and more in your spacesuit for free.
You can also not organize when the defence drones should launch, and when they shall not launch. And since they launch in singles over your small ship launchpad (4 times smaller, why not launch 4 or six at once?) only when an enemy apears, it get's kinda confusing. Imagine up to 16 drones blocking your view on a ceberus, because the cargo door keeps opening and closing for every single drone.... I am not looking forward to a carrier. Protecting a ships means to the AI that the wing will fire torps/missles even on the softest targets (civil smuglers).

Where is the key to turn a piece in the station planning screen?

All in all, it's so much hassle that you prefer to stick to the clean and simple gun boats.

And finally I really would like to thank you for an oportunity to get a lot more information out of the game by using your site instead of the bleak and uninformative in game sources. Splitting game windows for 1-4 more lines of text?

After years of reading about it and not having the desire or motive to actually steal one, I did. I thought that I would end up being kill on sight to most Paranids, but I only dropped a couple of ranks in rep, and soon made that back to max again after doing some missions for them.

What an amazing little ship, just fast enough (out the box) to be usable as a personal ship, with enough shields and weapons options to suit most gameplay styles. Then I went back and nicked an Agamemnon. I was soon back up to top rank with the Paranid though.

Now I've got 3 Vanguards in my space-armada.
