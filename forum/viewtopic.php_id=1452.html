End Of Line



Posted 5 years ago.

BigVern


Well in the end it just got too frustrating. Trying to do the mission where you need to get 5 x Xenon components. Despite repeated excursions into Xenon occupied space and spending a fortune on missiles and hull repairs, not to mention the tedium of the long trek back to civilisation, I didn't yield a single required item. Taking on 6 x M's in the Skunk is an almost impossible task, even after going to "Easy" level and manipulating my Argon rating to buy Level 4 shields. And forget going anywhere near a Xenon cap ship in the Skunk, ripped apart in seconds. So decided to pull the plug, uninstalled and shredded the save files.

Nice try Egosoft but in the end the game failed on so many levels. In X3, before doing that sort of mission you would be flying an M6 at the least, with fighter support and maybe the Boreas on standby to jump in and help out. The whole concept seemed flawed though, rushed and borked. The basis of the Universe to list just one example - here we have four star systems cut off from each other for years. Yet they all seem to have developed have the same lane travel, nearly identical space stations, the tech from one system is identical to that in another, the list goes on.

If they ever do another X game, hopefully lessons will be learned and the developer will return to an updated version of the tried and trusted gameplay and campaign system of previous games.
